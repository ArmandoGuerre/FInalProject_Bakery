The concha is arguably the most iconic Mexican baked good. There are 3 flavors of The Concha: chocolate, strawberry, and vanilla! They go well with a side of hot chocolate or coffee in the morning.

×

The word cochinito means” little pig” in Spanish. The cochinito is a molasses cookie shaped into a pig and typically glazed with cinnamon butter. They’re our most sold baked good here at Guerrero’s bakery and will be our first recommendation for you if your haven’t tried any Mexican backed good!

×

Bolio is the typical bread loaf that would be found on the streets and bakeries of Mexico. Many families use them as their loaf for sandwiches, tortas, or even make their own “Molletes” to make a cheap alternative meal for the family. This recipe has been passed down for a very long time so we guarantee you will get a piece of our culture with every bite!

×

What is flan? Flan is a baked sweet caramel custard, cooked to perfection. Many confuse it with Jellotin, but once you bite into it, you'll be pleasantly surprised with the sweet taste of a caramel tart. We sell 8,10, or 14-inch rounds or upon special request, we could cater and create one big flan cake for your desired occasion!
